What teaching activity recording options are there?

  1. Home
  2. Information and services
With lectures (and some other class activities) not being allocated to an on-campus location, lectures will not be automatically recorded with Echo360. A range of options for recording lectures and teaching activities are available.
 

Dual mode lectures using Zoom

Zoom is the best way to record lectures and other teaching activities where dual teaching is done using Zoom.
 
It is recommended Zoom meetings have the 'Automatically record meeting' 'In the cloud' option set in the advanced options when scheduling the teaching activity. See Organising a Zoom meeting for more details.
 
 
Zoom recordings can be made available to students through Blackboard by adding the Zoom recording to Learn.UQ.
 

Teaching activities scheduled in an Echo360 location

If your teaching activity is scheduled with an Echo360 location, recordings can be automatically scheduled.
 
To have your activities scheduled for automatic recording, contact your timetable coordinator who can mark them to be recorded in the Syllabus Plus timetable system.
 
Your recording will be automatically published to your course Blackboard site on Learn.UQ.
 

Activities scheduled without a location

If you are teaching using Zoom, it is recommended Zoom be used for recording. See the above section 'Dual mode lectures using Zoom'.
