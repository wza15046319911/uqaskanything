How do I save my OneDrive files?

  1. Home
  2. Information and services

Considerations

  • OneDrive data can be saved from:
    • OneDrive online (All platforms)
    • OneDrive Desktop app (Windows or Mac)
  • It is recommended that data is saved via the OneDrive desktop app to reduce interruptions and failures.
  • Ensure you have enough space on your device to store the downloaded data. Check how much data you have.
  • Allocate a significant amount of time for this process if you have large amounts of data in your OneDrive account.
  • Files can be downloaded to Android and iOS devices, but this is not recommended.

OneDrive Desktop App (Windows and Mac)

  1. Install and log in to the OneDrive desktop app.
  2. Sync all your online files to the desktop app (may take some time).
    • For Windows: Click on the cloud icon > Settings cog > Setting > Sync and backup > Files On-Demand > Download all files.
    • For Mac: Click on the cloud icon > Preferences > Preferences tab > Files On-Demand > Download all OneDrive files now.
  3. Ensure ALL your files have been downloaded to your computer. This is indicated by a checkmark beside each file.
  4. Select the folders or files you want to keep, right click on the selection and then select copy.
  5. Open the drive storage, or external device, where you wish to move the data, and right click to paste.

OneDrive Online (All platforms)

  1. Log into your OneDrive online account > Select My files.
  2. Select the check boxes of the file or files that you want to download. You can select all files by clicking the checkbox in the row heading.
  3. Click Download. Depending on the amount of data it may take some time for all files to be downloaded.
  4. All files selected will be downloaded to the downloads location specified on your computer (usually the Downloads folder).
Note: If you select multiple files, they will download together as a single zipped folder.
