How many courses do I have to take on exchange?

  1. Home
  2. Information and services

The number of courses that you need to take while on exchange at your host university could vary between 2-12 individual courses. Each host university is quite different, so it's important to understand the full-time study load required by your host. 

 

You can find the full-time study load for each of the host universities on the individual host university page. The full-time study load is usually listed in 'credits', 'ECTS', 'hours', 'units' or 'courses'. 

 

To transfer a full-time study load (8 UQ units) of credit back to your UQ degree, you must successfully complete the full-time study load listed for your host university.

 

For example: 

  • A full-time study load at Purdue University, USA is 15 credits per semester. You must complete 15 credits of approved study at Purdue to gain 8 units of credit back at UQ. This is generally achieved by completing 5 Purdue courses worth 3 credit points each. 

  • A full-time study load at University College Dublin, Ireland is 30 ECTS per semester. You must complete 30 ECTS of approved study at University College Dublin to gain 8 units of credit back at UQ. This is generally achieved by completing 6 UCD courses worth 5 ECTS each.
