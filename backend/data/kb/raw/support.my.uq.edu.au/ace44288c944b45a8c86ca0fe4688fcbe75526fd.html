Lectern PC Web Sign-In experience

  1. Home
  2. Information and services
UQ has introduced a new sign-in experience for Lectern PCs called Web Sign-In. This feature lets UQ staff log in using their UQ username and password, and includes Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) as part of the login process.
 
Using Web Sign-In allows applications such as Microsoft Teams, Office, and Microsoft Edge to sign in without further MFA prompts for a more streamlined login experience.
 

Sign in to Lectern PCs

UQ Staff

  1. Select Sign in to uq.edu.au


     
  2. You will receive a UQ Authenticate pop-up. Enter your UQ username and password and complete the multi-factor authentication process.

    Note: With the transition from UQ Auth to Okta in progress, it is expected behaviour to be required to type your username and password twice before you can MFA. Once into UQ Auth, and once again into Okta. This will be streamlined once the transition has been finalised.

 

UQ Guests and Visitors

  1. On the sign-in screen, select Guest on the bottom left, then select Sign in in the middle of the screen.


     
  2. To gain access to the internet, you will need to be supplied with a UQ visitor account.
    You may access the internet by launching the Internet Access desktop shortcut.


     
  3. Log in with your visitor account credentials.


     

FAQs (Frequently asked questions)

The lectern has synced all of my files on my desktop from OneDrive and there is confidential material there. How could this be prevented?
There are 2 options to overcome this.
Option 1: Login to the device as a guest user. This will no longer give you the new sign in experience and you will be required to manually sign into Office, Teams, OneDrive and or the internet to access work resources.
Option 2: Save any confidential work into the documents folder in OneDrive not on the desktop.

What happens to all my synced OneDrive data after I use the lectern?
After you log off the device it will automatically delete your profile cleaning up anything left on that device.

I used to use the lecture account to login?
This has been replaced with the guest account on the login screen.
 
Is there an automatic logout period?
The device will auto lock after 30 minutes of inactivity.

Why do I have to type my username and password in twice to log in with MFA?
With the migration from UQ Auth and Duo to Okta for MFA, there is currently a known issue where users need to sign into UQ Auth and then another time into the Okta window to MFA. This will be streamlined as the Okta authentication is fully cutover from UQ Auth.
 
Why do I need to sign in with MFA again to access Blackboard or Zoom?
Because these systems work with a slightly different authentication flow, they are unable to use the existing MFA you used to log in to the lectern. This is a known concern, and we are working to streamline this process.
