Setting up Okta MFA

  1. Home
  2. Information and services

UQ is progressively moving to Okta as the University's new Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) service from early to mid-2026. Okta will not be available on your account until you receive your personalised activation email.

How do I complete my initial MFA setup with Okta?

When you’re invited to set up with UQ’s Okta MFA app, you have a number of ways to get started depending on your account type – student, staff or part of our broader UQ community (alumni and past students).

Students, alumni and past students - choose whichever option suits, including:

  1. Okta Verify - on your mobile phone or computer (see set up steps below)
  2. Synced (or 'roaming') passkeys - such as Apple iCloud Keychain, Google Password Manager, Edge Password Manager or Samsung Pass
  3. Google Authenticator or similar app - generates a code on your preferred TOTP app
  4. Using your own YubiKey - once Okta Verify is set up.
Staff must start with setting up Okta Verify – either on your mobile phone or computer. Once that is set up, you can use:
  1. Okta Verify – on your mobile phone or computer (see set up steps below)
  2. Synced (or 'roaming') passkeys - optional once Okta Verify is set up, using e.g. Google Password Manager, Apple iCloud Keychain
  3. Using your own YubiKey - optional once Okta Verify is set up
  4. Temporary access code (TAC) - for one-off situations where your usual method is unavailable.
Note: You will not be asked to purchase the Okta Verify app. If you are prompted to purchase the app, please ensure you have selected the right one.
 
While UQ transitions to Okta, please keep Duo installed on your mobile phone until you are told it is no longer needed. A small number of UQ systems will still use Duo until the transition is complete.
 

Option 1: Set up using your mobile phone

You’ll receive a ‘Welcome to Okta – activate your account’ email from UQ. This includes a unique activation link to begin setup.
 
You'll need:
  • Your UQ username and password (Students: find out what your UQ username is)
  • Internet access
  • A compatible mobile phone (iOS or Android). If you do not want to use your personal mobile phone for MFA, use the ‘Option 2: Set up directly on your computer’ instructions.
If you do not want to use your personal mobile phone for MFA, use Option 2 - 'Set up directly on your computer' instructions.
 
To activate:
  1. Open the ‘Welcome to Okta – activate your account’ email.
  2. Select the ‘Activate your Okta account’ button.
  3. Download and install Okta Verify on your mobile phone from the App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android).
  4. Open the Okta Verify app and follow the instructions.
  5. ‘Scan the QR code’ shown on your browser to link your account. See ‘What if I can’t scan the Okta QR code?’ if needed and follow the prompts.
  6. Once complete, your mobile device is registered with MFA.
View a Quick Reference Guide
 

Option 2: Set up directly on your computer (optional)

You can also set up Okta Verify directly on your laptop or desktop. This enables Okta Verify's FastPass feature, which uses your device's built in security (Windows Hello or Apple Touch ID) to reduce MFA prompts.
 
Before you start:
  • Set up Windows Hello (Windows - staff login required), view instructions for students, or
  • Set up Apple Touch ID (Mac) on your computer.
To activate:
  1. Log into the computer you want to activate Okta Verify on.
  2. Open your ‘Welcome to Okta - activate your account' email and click on the activation link.
  3. Open the Okta Verify app on your laptop or desktop:
    • UQ-managed computers - if you can’t find Okta Verify using your computer ‘search’, installation instructions for Windows or Apple can be found here.
    • Personal computers - download Okta Verify and install. View a Quick Reference Guide for Okta download options.
  4. Open Okta Verify and select 'Get started'.
  5. Enter https://signin.uq.edu.au/ (UQ-managed computers will automatically have this shown).
  6. Sign in with your UQ username and password. Approve the prompt.
  7. Click Enable when prompted if you want to enable Windows Hello or Apple TouchID. If you don’t complete this step, you will not be able to use Okta Verify’s FastPass feature.
Once set up, Okta Verify's FastPass is used automatically for MFA on that device. For more information on the difference between Okta, Okta Verify and FastPass refer to this FAQ.
 
Passkeys and hardware tokens (such as YubiKeys) may be available as alternative MFA options, depending on your account - see Can I use passkeys for Okta MFA? or Can I use a hardware token or ‘dongle’ for Okta MFA? for setup requirements.
 
View a Quick Reference Guide
 
Note: Even if you use FastPass on a UQ-managed device, you should keep Okta Verify installed on your mobile device or smartphone. You may need it again if you switch devices, access UQ systems from another non-FastPass enrolled computer or are asked to re-verify your identity for security reasons.
 

How do I set up synced passkeys for Okta MFA?

If you want to use a synced (or ‘roaming’) passkey, you will need to set one up in your password manager of choice (e.g. iCloud Keychain or Google Password Manager), before following the steps below. This is something you do outside of Okta, in your own time, using your personal device or account. Once set up, Okta will detect it automatically - it won't create one for you.
 
Staff - you must enable Okta Verify on your phone or computer before adding a passkey to your account.
 
Set up a synced (or 'roaming') passkey using the following steps:
  1. Sign into your Okta ‘My Settings’ dashboard (https://signin.uq.edu.au/account-settings/home)
  2. Click on ‘Security methods’, ‘Manage Security methods’ button.
  3. Click on the 'Passkeys', ‘+ Set up’ button.
  4. Follow the prompts.
  5. Your device will automatically discover your browser-stored passkeys (e.g. Google Password Manager, iCloud Keychain).
  6. You will be prompted to verify your identity, and your passkey will be enabled.
 

How do I set up Google Authenticator or other TOTP authenticator apps?

UQ students, alumni and past students may use Google Authenticator or other preferred TOTP apps, in place of Okta Verify.
 
Before you start:
  1. You can select as part of your initial activation – by opening your 'Welcome to Okta - activate your account' email and select Activate your Okta account
  2. Or afterwards - by going to your Okta dashboard’s security methods, and select + Set up under Google Authenticator (https://signin.uq.edu.au/account-settings/security)
Then use the following steps when prompted to set up an authenticator:
  1. Select Google Authenticator. Despite the name, you can use any compatible authenticator app in the next step.
  2. Open your authenticator app, tap the + icon
  3. Select Scan a QR code
  4. Scan the QR code shown on your screen. Your account will be added automatically.
  5. If you can't scan the QR code (i.e you are already following these steps on your phone), select Can't scan? on screen and enter the setup key shown into your app manually instead.
  6. Enter the six-digit code generated by your app to verify setup is complete.
View a Quick Reference Guide
 
 

How do I set up Okta MFA with a self-use YubiKey?

These are instructions to set up self-use YubiKeys only. Staff can purchase YubiKeys via UQeMarket. This is not the same process as using a UQ-lab assigned YubiKey.
 
The setup and ongoing use steps follow the general flow. Some prompts will look different depending on whether you are using Windows, macOS, or Linux. Follow the on-screen prompts.
 

Before you start

  • Okta Verify is already set up on either your mobile phone or computer.
  • If your YubiKey already has a PIN, have it ready. If not, you will create one during setup. This PIN is unique to your YubiKey and separate from any other passwords or PINs you use. Keep a note of it, as you will need it each time you use your YubiKey.
  • If you need to change or reset your YubiKey PIN, follow the Yubico manufacturer's instructions, before registering it for Okta MFA.

Initial setup

  1. Connect the YubiKey to the device (computer / tablet).
  2. On your computer, go to your Security Methods on your Okta ‘My Settings’ dashboard (https://signin.uq.edu.au/account-settings/security)
  3. Click on the Set up link under the ‘Security Key or Biometric Authenticator’ section. You will be prompted to verify your identity via MFA again.
  4. On the ‘Set up security key or biometric authenticator’ pop-up, click on the Set up button.
  5. On prompt to 'Save your passkey', it will store on your computer by default. To store it on your YubiKey, plug in your YubiKey and select Change.
    Windows only: Windows may prompt you to use Windows Hello (your device PIN, face ID or fingerprint) first. If this happens, select Use a different device or More options to continue with your YubiKey.
  6. On prompt to 'Choose where to save your passkey', select Security key.
  7. On prompt to ‘Enter your security key PIN’, enter a PIN of your choice that is at least 4 digits and click OK.
    • First time using your YubiKey: create a PIN of at least 4 characters, confirm it, and click OK.
    • YubiKey used before: enter your existing PIN and click OK.
  8. Touch your YubiKey (gold logo) - this is a presence check to confirm it is not being done remotely (this is not a biometric scan).
  9. You will now see your YubiKey registered under your Okta ‘Security Key or Biometric Authenticator’ items and receive a ‘MFA device activated’ email, by way of confirmation.

Ongoing use of your Okta registered YubiKey

When you next sign in with Okta using your YubiKey, you can use your PIN.
If you have Okta Verify setup on your computer, it will attempt a FastPass MFA login using biometrics. Cancel that request first, before using your YubiKey.
  1. Connect the YubiKey to the device (computer/tablet).
  2. Go to https://signin.uq.edu.au and enter your username. Click Next.
  3. Click Verify with something else and then select ‘Security Key or Biometric Authenticator’.
  4. Select Security Key.
  5. Enter your PIN on the Sign in with a Passkey prompt and select OK.
  6. Touch your YubiKey (gold logo) - this is a presence check to confirm it is not being done remotely, not a biometric scan.
  7. You are now logged in.
View a Quick Reference Guide
 
Note: If you have a token with a generated code (e.g. Duo dongle), it is not compatible with Okta Verify. You will need to purchase a YubiKey or FIDO2 standard token.
 
Staff who use hardware tokens because they prefer not to use a personal mobile phone for MFA can alternatively use Okta Verify (FastPass) on their UQ-managed desktop. This lets you sign in using your desktop’s built in security (e.g. Windows Hello or Apple Touch ID). No personal device or hardware token is required. For instructions on how to register your UQ-managed laptop or desktop, refer to ‘How to install Okta Verify on your laptop or desktop’.
 

How do I activate MFA for multiple accounts?

If you have both a student and staff account, you will need to set up Okta MFA for both accounts. You will receive a ‘Welcome to Okta’ email for each account  (e.g. if you are both a staff and student at UQ, you will receive an email for each to that account’s email address).
 
When setting up MFA on your second account, your browser will need to be logged in with the account that requires MFA activation. To ensure you are logged in with the correct account, use one of the following solutions:
  1. Open a private or incognito browsing window.
    Set up a separate profile in your web browser, by following these instructions for Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge.
  2. Open a different web browser than you would normally use (e.g. Firefox, Chrome, Edge).
    Using the new browser window, follow the FAQ instructions ‘How do I complete my initial MFA setup with Okta?’.
Okta supports using the same phone number on multiple UQ accounts.
 
View a Quick Reference Guide
 
 

How do I register a new phone or device for Okta MFA?

If you need to register a new or additional mobile device, follow these steps from your laptop or desktop. You will be prompted to sign in.
  1. Sign in to your Okta Dashboard (https://signin.uq.edu.au)
  2. Go to your 'My Settings' menu (click on this link, or find under your Okta profile top right dropdown).
  3. Select ‘Security methods’ from the left-hand menu.
  4. Under the ‘Okta Verify’ section, select ‘+ Set up another’. This will allow you to register a new mobile device.
  5. Enter your UQ account password when prompted.
  6. After successful confirmation of your password you will see a ‘Set up security methods’ setup assistant.
  7. Select ‘Okta Verify -> Set up now’.
  8. Download Okta Verify on your new mobile device from the App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android).
  9. Open the Okta Verify app and follow the instructions. When prompted, scan the QR code shown on your computer. You have now successfully linked your account to your registered device.
  10. You will receive an email confirming you have successfully activated a new device.
  11. If there are any devices listed under ‘Security methods’ that you no longer use, select ‘Remove’ so it can no longer be used to access your account.
View a Quick Reference Guide
 
 
If you have lost, damaged or temporarily misplaced or forgotten the phone you have registered for Okta MFA, contact ITS Service Desk for assistance.
 

How do I remove a phone or registered device from Okta Verify?

Go to your 'My Settings' menu (available under your Okta profile, top right dropdown).
  1. Select ‘Security methods’ from the left-hand menu.
  2. Under the ‘Okta Verify’ section, click on the ‘Remove’ button beside the device you want to deregister.
  3. You will be prompted to confirm you want to continue with de-registration of Okta Verify for that device. Click on ‘Yes’.
  4. Okta Verify will send you a push notification to confirm that you are initiating the action. Click ‘Yes, it’s me’ and complete the prompts.
  5. You have now successfully removed your device from Okta Verify.
  6. You will receive an email confirming you have deactivated your device.
If you have lost, damaged or temporarily misplaced or forgotten the phone you have registered for Okta MFA, contact ITS Service Desk for assistance.
 

How do I MFA in a location without mobile coverage?

If you are accessing UQ systems and cannot get mobile coverage for MFA, use the following steps to generate a one-time temporary code (i.e. OTP).
 
When logging into Okta:
  1. Enter your username.
  2. When prompted for MFA click the 'Verify with something else' link.
  3. Click the 'Okta Verify Enter a code' button.
  4. Open the Okta Verify app on your phone.
  5. Click the  icon in the Okta Verify app, making note of the 6-digit code.
  6. Enter the 6-digit code in your web browser.
View a Quick Reference Guide
 
 

How do I generate a temporary access code for MFA?

You can generate a temporary MFA passcode for a limited period, e.g. up to 48 hrs. Self-service temporary access codes (TACs) are available on your Okta user dashboard.
 
Note: Okta Temporary Access Codes replace Duo Bypass Codes.
 
To generate a TAC:
  1. Sign in to your Okta Dashboard (https://signin.uq.edu.au)
  2. Select the 'Request access' button under 'My Apps'
  3. Select the 'Request Temporary Access Code' tile
  4. Follow the prompts to select the length of time you require (maximum 48hrs)
  5. Scroll down to the Chat section of the screen to view your temporary access code. You will also receive an 'Okta Access Requests' email containing this code.
  6. Use the code when prompted to MFA.
View a Quick Reference Guide
 
Note: your TAC is valid starting immediately and will expire based on the length of time you have selected.
 
If you have lost, damaged or temporarily misplaced or forgotten the phone registered for Okta MFA or you have forgotten your Windows Hello PIN, contact ITS Service Desk for assistance.
 

What if I can't scan the Okta QR code?

If you’re unable to scan the QR code during set up:
  1. Click on the ‘Can’t Scan?’ button underneath the QR code image.
  2. You’ll be prompted to provide either your email or text for your registration link. We recommend using the text method for the mobile device you are registering, for a more seamless experience.
  3. If you select ‘text’, you will receive a text with a link. Click on the ‘Activate your Okta account’ link in the text. The link in the text expires after a short time, so be sure to complete the activation promptly.
  4. If you select ‘email’, enter your UQ email. You will receive an email titled ‘Okta Verify Push registration’. Click on the ‘Activate your Okta account’ link. The link in the email expires after a short time, so be sure to complete the activation promptly.
  5. For both methods, clicking on the link will open the Okta Verify app (if installed). If it’s not installed, you’ll be directed to download it first.
  6. When the app opens, follow the prompts to enable account activation. Tap ‘Done’ to complete the registration.
  7. Once activated, return to your browser to finalise any remaining steps.
View a Quick Reference Guide
 
 

What is Windows Hello and how do I activate it?

Windows Hello is required for Okta FastPass use on Windows devices. Windows Hello is a feature available in Windows 10 and Windows 11 that allows users to sign in to their devices without using a traditional password. Instead, it utilises biometric authentication methods, such as:
  • Facial Recognition: Uses an infrared camera to recognise your face.
  • Fingerprint Recognition: Requires a compatible fingerprint reader.
  • PIN: A numeric code that serves as an alternative to passwords.
View instructions for setting up Windows Hello (staff login required)
View instructions for students
 
Note: The biometric data used to support Windows Hello is stored on the local device only. It doesn't roam and is never sent to external devices or servers. This separation helps to stop potential attackers by providing no single collection point that an attacker could potentially compromise to steal biometric data. Even if an attacker could obtain the biometric data from a device, it couldn't be converted back into a raw biometric sample recognisable by the biometric sensor.
 

Why am I receiving prompts for Duo?

During the transition to Okta, certain applications will continue to use Duo as an Identity Provider and Multifactor Provider (see list below). For most other UQ platforms, Okta will handle multifactor authentication.
 
Applications that will continue to use Duo through to 2026:
  • Remote Access (RDP) for ITS and EAIT
  • Remote Linux Command Line (SSH) for IMB, RCC-HPC and EAIT
  • VPN connections using SBL
 

Can I use my Apple Watch for MFA with Okta Verify?

At the moment, no. Apple Watch cannot currently be used with Okta Verify to approve MFA requests. You’ll need to use your iPhone for authentication.
 
The current MFA policy requires a biometric (something you are) to confirm the verification. Okta treats both biometrics (Face ID / Touch ID) and a device PIN as valid options.
  • On iPhone: this works as expected - you’ll see a Face ID/Touch ID prompt or be asked for your PIN.
  • On Apple Watch, however, the PIN prompt isn’t passed through to Okta. As a result, the verification response fails and the authentication can’t complete.
 

Can I set up MFA if my device is not compatible with Okta, or if I want to use another TOTP app?

While we understand staff may have a preference for a different authentication app or prefer not to use a personal phone, there are several options available to you:
  1. Install Okta Verify on your computer: You don't need a mobile phone to use Okta Verify when installed on your computer. This is particularly recommended for UQ staff.
  2. Use a passkey: Passkeys are a secure, password-free way to verify your identity and are supported on most modern devices. See How do I set up synced passkeys for Okta MFA?
  3. Use a YubiKey: A YubiKey is a small physical security key that plugs into your device. See How do I set up Okta MFA with a self-use YubiKey?
If you are concerned your current device isn’t compatible, refer here for a list of Android and Apple operating systems and internet browsers currently supported by Okta.
 
If you are a student or part of our broader UQ community, you are able to set up Google Authenticator or similar app, which generates a code on your preferred TOTP app.
 
As a staff member, if you’re unable to use any of these options, or your device isn’t on the Okta-supported list, you may need to apply for a ‘TOTP exemption’. 
 
To apply for a TOTP exemption:
  1. Sign in to your Okta Dashboard (https://signin.uq.edu.au)
  2. Select the 'Request access' button under 'My apps'.
  3. Select the 'Request Exemption - Allow ToTP' tile
  4. Click the 'Request access' link
  5. Complete the 'Please outline the reason why this exemption is required' and 'How long is it required for?' fields
  6. Click 'Submit new request'.
You will receive an email within two business days from UQ’s Cyber Security Risk and Assurance team to advise you as to whether the request has been approved or rejected.
 
You can view your request history in your Okta ‘Requests’ dashboard.
 

My 'Welcome to Okta' email activation link has expired. What should I do?

If you did not activate your Okta account using the link in your email within 90 days, the link will have expired. This will not stop you from setting up Okta Verify.
 
You will be prompted to set it up the next time you log in to a UQ system. When prompted, follow the 'Set up security methods' steps — you do not need to use the activation link in your 'Welcome to Okta' email.
 
The activation link is provided as a convenient way to get started, but it is not the only way to set up your account.
