You're a domestic student if you are:

  • a citizen of Australia or New Zealand,
  • an Australian permanent resident, or
  • a holder of an Australian permanent humanitarian visa.

You're an international student if you are:

  • intending to study on a student visa,
  • not a citizen of Australia or New Zealand,
  • not an Australian permanent resident, or
  • a temporary resident (visa status) of Australia.

Minimum entry scores for Doctor of Medicine (provisional entry)

School leavers applying for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine program in 2026 will need a minimum adjusted ATAR (Australian Tertiary Admissions Rank) of 95, or an equivalent score from another high school qualification.

Select your qualification from the list below to find your ATAR equivalent score. 

You need to attain a score of 85.56 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on: 

  • OSSD: Grade average of best 6 University (U, 4U) and/or University/College (U/C, 4M) courses
  • Alberta High School Diploma: Grade average of best 5 Level 30 Provincial Diploma Exams
  • British Columbia Senior Secondary School Graduation Diploma: grade average using the Language Arts 12 subject (provincially examined if prior to 2020) plus 3 Grade 12 subjects with 4 credit points.

The following minimum entry scores are also accepted:

  • Manitoba: grade average of best 5 grade 12 subjects
  • New Brunswick: grade average of best 5 grade 12 subjects
  • Newfoundland and Labrador: grade average of best 5 grade 12 results
  • Nova Scotia: grade average of best 5 grade 12 subjects – level 3 (grade 12) or 4 (advanced placement) subjects
  • Prince Edward Island: grade average of best 5 grade 12 subjects. Enriched/Advanced or Academic subjects only
  • Saskatchewan: grade average of best 5 grade 12 results, level 30 subjects and advanced subjects.

You need to attain a score of 9.5 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on the average of your final year results converted to a 13-point scale, ranging from 00 (lowest) to 12 (highest), with a passing mark set at 2.

You need to attain a score of 15 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on your average score, with 10 as the pass mark.

You need to attain a score of 1.8 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on the reformed upper secondary level (oberstufe), the Durchschnittsnote = 5 2/3 – P/180 where P = Punktzahl der Gesamtqualifikation. If P is greater than 840, Durchschnittsnote = 1. 1 (sehr gut), 2 (gut), 3 (befriedigend), 4 (ausreichend).

You need to attain a score of 25 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on the combined total of the best 5 subjects (Category A, B or C), where:

  • 5*, 5** = 6
  • 5 = 5
  • 4 = 4
  • 3 = 3
  • 2 = 2
  • 1 = 1
  • A* = 6
  • A = 5
  • B = 4
  • C = 3
  • D = 2
  • E = 1
  • Attained with Distinction = 3
  • Attained = 1. 

You need to attain a score of 97.5 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on the grade average of your best 4 subjects (converted to a percentage scale).

You need to attain a score of 92.5 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on the grade average of your best 4 subjects (converted into a percentage scale). 

You need to attain a score of 37.5 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

You need a score of 9.69 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on the grade average of your final year results with a minimum 7 subjects where:

  • A = 12
  • A- = 11
  • B+ = 10
  • B = 9
  • B- = 8
  • C + = 7
  • C = 6
  • C- = 5
  • D+ = 4
  • D = 3
  • D- = 2
  • E = 1.

You need a score of 368 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine. 

The score is based on:

  • CSAT score must include the following subjects: Korean Language, Mathematics (either stream) and the best of 2 social science or science subjects or a combination of both
  • completion of the Republic of Korea high school diploma.

You need a score of 11.33 for entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on the aggregate of your best 3 subjects, where:

  • A = 4.00
  • A- = 3.67
  • B+ = 3.33
  • B = 3.00
  • B- = 2.67
  • C+ = 2.33
  • C = 2.00.

You need a score of 3.6 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine. 

The score is based on the average of your best 3 subjects, where:

  • A = 4.00
  • A- = 3.67
  • B+ = 3.33
  • B = 3.00
  • B- = 2.67
  • C+ = 2.33
  • C = 2.00 (pass)
  • C- = 1.67
  • D+ = 1.33
  • D = 1.00
  • F = 0.00.

You need a score of 5 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on the aggregate of your best 5 academic subjects where:

  • A1 = 1
  • A2 = 2
  • B3 = 3
  • B4 = 4
  • B5 = 5
  • B6 = 6.

You need a score of 5.1 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on the grade average of your final year results from your Norwegian Certificate of Completion of Upper Secondary School Examinations or Comparable Qualification.

You need a score of 80.6 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on the grade average of your final year results where:

  • Pass = 33% (E)
  • Satisfactory = 40% (D)
  • Good = 50% (C)
  • Very Good = 60% (B)
  • Excellent = 70% (A)
  • Outstanding = 80% (A-1).

You need a score of 14 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on the aggregate of your best 3 H2 subjects and your best H1 subject. For H2 subjects:

  • A = 5
  • B = 4
  • C = 3
  • D = 2
  • E = 1.

For H1 subjects, the notional value is half of that assigned to H2 subjects.

You need a score of 6.13 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine. 

The score is based on the grade average of your best 5 subjects in your final year (excluding the subject Life Orientation and Mathematics (Third Paper)), where:

  • 7 = 80–100%
  • 6 = 70–79%
  • 5 = 60–69%
  • 4 = 50–59%
  • 3 = 40–49%
  • 2 = 30–39%
  • 1 = 0–29%.

You need a score of 3.13 for entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on the grade average of your best 3 A Level subjects where:

  • A = 4
  • B = 3
  • C = 2
  • S = 1.

Results in the Common General Test, General English Paper, Group Project or Individual Project are not used in the calculation of the grade average.

You need a score of 3.8 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine. 

The score is based on the grade average of your final year results, where:

  • A = 5
  • B = 4
  • C = 3
  • D = 2
  • E = 1
  • F = 0.

You need a score of 52 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on the aggregate of your best 4 subjects, with the scaled score in each subject ranging from 0 to 1.

You need to attain a score of 14 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.  

The score is based on the aggregate of your best 3 (or 2) A Level subjects or a combination of A and AS Level subjects equivalent to 3 (or 2) A Level subjects where:

  • A* = 6
  • A = 5
  • B = 4
  • C = 3
  • D = 2,
  • E = 1

AS Levels count for half of these scores.

This score applies to ‘A’ Levels for locations including:

  • Bahamas
  • Bangladesh
  • Brunei
  • Cameroon
  • Falkland Islands
  • Gambia, Ghana
  • Gibraltar
  • Hong Kong (SAR)
  • Ireland
  • Kenya
  • Liberia
  • Macau
  • Malawi
  • Malta
  • Mauritius
  • Namibia
  • Nigeria
  • Seychelles
  • Sierra Leone
  • Tanzania
  • Uganda
  • United Kingdom
  • Zambia
  • Zimbabwe.

You need a score of 1380 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on the total of your SAT-I: Evidence-Based Reading and Writing and SAT-I: Mathematics. You must also provide evidence of a recognised school leaving certificate.

You need a score of 32 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine. 

Students must also provide evidence of a recognised school leaving certificate.

You need to attain a score of 16 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The scores is based on a minimum of 2 and a maximum of 4 APs.

The aggregate is determined where:

  • A = 5
  • B = 4
  • C = 3 (Pass)
  • D = 2
  • E = 1.

If you took more than 4 AP exams, the best 4 are used. You must also provide evidence of a recognised school leaving certificate.

You need a score of 9 for provisional entry into the Doctor of Medicine.

The score is based on the average of your final year results.
